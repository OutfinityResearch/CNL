Supported Ontologies

CNL-PL supports importing standard Semantic Web ontologies to bootstrap domain knowledge. This page documents the vendored ontologies available for import and their intended usage scenarios.

Core Theories Ecosystem

The CNL project provides specialized theory bundles tailored for specific discourse levels and domains.

theories/base.cnl

Goal: Everyday Discourse.

For casual conversation, light literature reading, and general knowledge. Defines common-sense concepts (People, Places, Time) without heavy academic rigor.

theories/base.formal.cnl

Goal: Pop-Science & Sci-Fi.

For popular science communication, science fiction writing, and structured explanations. Adds rigorous measurements, units, and physical laws to the base.

theories/legal.cnl

Goal: Juridical Documents.

For processing legal texts, norms, and contracts. Built on LKIF-Core to handle rights, obligations, and normative statements.

theories/literature.cnl

Goal: Literary Analysis & Quality.

For literary criticism, style analysis, and documentation assessment. Built on FRBR and FaBiO to model works, expressions, and bibliographic metadata.

Foundational Ontologies

These provide the upper-level structure for modeling reality.

BFO (Basic Formal Ontology)

Rigorous distinction between continuants (objects) and occurrents (processes). Best for scientific and engineering domains.

DUL (DOLCE+DnS Ultra Lite)

Models physical and social reality, including descriptions, situations, and plans. Great for complex social/cognitive modeling.

Domain & Utility Ontologies

RO (Relation Ontology)

The standard library of relationship types (part_of, located_in, derives_from) with defined logical properties.

IAO (Information Artifact Ontology)

For modeling documents, data sets, algorithms, and metadata.

OWL-Time

W3C standard for temporal intervals, instants, and durations.

GeoSPARQL

OGC standard for spatial objects, geometries, and topological reasoning.

PROV-O

W3C standard for provenance (activity, agent, entity derivation).

FOAF

Social networking: people, groups, and basic profiles.

FRBR

Bibliographic records: Work, Expression, Manifestation, Item.

FaBiO

Bibliographic ontology compatible with FRBR, for publishing.

LKIF-Core

Legal norms, rights, powers, and normative statements.

WGS84 Geo

Simple latitude/longitude point tagging.

Example & Mini Ontologies

Small, custom ontologies used for testing and tutorials.

Science Mini

Basic physical objects, atoms, and molecules.

Semantic Web Mini

Agents, claims, and beliefs.
