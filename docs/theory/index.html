Theory

Controlled language as executable logic.

CNL-PL formalizes natural language into a symbolic representation that can be executed, queried, proven, planned, or optimized without ambiguity.

The SVO Model (Subject-Verb-Object)

At the heart of CNL-PL is the deterministic mapping of sentences to SVO triplets. Unlike natural English where meaning can be fluid, CNL rigidifies this structure.

Every assertion, no matter how complex, breaks down into these three components (or a copula variant like "X is Y").

Pragmatic engines: One Input, Many Outputs

The power of CNL-PL lies in separating the representation (the sentence) from the intent (the pragmatic command).

Query

Input: "Return the name of every user who is active."

Output: ["Alice", "Bob", "Charlie"] (List of Strings)

Verify

Input: "Verify that Alice is active."

Output: true (Boolean)

Plan

Input: "Plan to achieve Alice is active."

Output: [Action("ActivateUser", "Alice")] (List of Steps)

Explain

Input: "Explain why Alice is active."

Output: "Alice is active because Rule #4 applied to Fact #12." (Causal Trace)

Why deterministic language matters

Unambiguous structure

Every valid sentence compiles to a single AST; there is no alternative parse.

Explicit grouping

Boolean logic uses explicit connectors to prevent hidden precedence.

Controlled vocabulary

Determiners and quantifiers separate names from noun phrases.

Design principles

  • Triplet assertions are the atomic unit of truth.
  • Relative clauses replace parentheses for human readability.
  • Explicit connectors remove hidden precedence in logic.
  • Grammar remains stable as pragmatics evolve.
